## Why we're doing this
We added capacity — **slots went from 50 → 70/month** for 2H — but enrollments have **dipped**, so the top of the funnel isn't feeding the new slots. Revenue lands at **infusion**, and the funnel leaks along the way (~100 enroll → −30% before TTP → −30% before infusion → ~50 infusions). The standing IC plan already rewards infusions; **this contest targets the enrollment step that feeds them**, for the two months where it matters most.

**Goal:** drive net-new enrollments in Aug–Sep — fairly across 24 uneven territories, without gaming the funnel.

## The scoring engine (shared by both options)

**One rule:** *score on one metric, guard with a few, watch the rest.*

| Job | Metric | How it's used |
|---|---|---|
| **SCORE** | Net-new enrollments **over baseline** | The only thing that decides rank |
| **GUARD** | Enrollment→TTP conversion · 7-day/LTE cancellations · unique-patient dedup | Keeps reps from stuffing the funnel with bad-fit patients |
| **WATCH** | TTPs · infusions · slot utilization | Confirms the contest helped the *business*, not just the number |

**Baseline** — each territory vs. **its own** trailing run-rate (≈ avg quarterly enrollments over the last 4 quarters, scaled to the 2-month window). Locked from a window that ends **before** we announce, so no one can sandbag.

**Buckets** — split the 24 territories into **3 groups of 8 by baseline size** (Large/Med/Small — see the data-driven split in *What the data says* above; Kolin's call is 2 or 3). This is the primary fairness control: reps only compete against peers their own size.

**The 50/50 blend** — within each bucket, rank on **absolute growth** and on **% growth** separately, then average the two ranks. Balances big and small territories exactly as Kolin framed it.

*Mini example (one bucket):*

| Rep | Baseline | Contest | Abs growth | % growth | Blend rank |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 20 | 26 | +6 | +30% | **1st 🏆** |
| B | 12 | 17 | +5 | +42% | **1st 🏆** |
| C | 6 | 9 | +3 | +50% | 3rd |

Big-absolute A and high-% B tie at the top — neither wins on size alone.

**Payout** — per the Daily Connect, **top 2 per group** (3 groups → 6 winners).

## Option A — "The Ladder" (lean)
A clean bucketed leaderboard. Growth over baseline, 50/50 blend, top 2 per group, TTP quality gate. One end-of-contest ranking.
- ✅ Simplest to run and explain · low admin · nothing to argue with.
- ⚠️ Pure winner-take-all can lose the middle of the pack early.

## Option B — "The Rally" (gamified — Kolin's "fun for the field")
Everything in A, reframed as a points game to keep energy across both months:
- **Points + quality multiplier:** 1 point per enrollment over baseline, **×1.5 if it reaches TTP** — turns the quality gate into a *reward*, not a filter.
- **Halftime + weekly movers:** a spot prize end-of-August and weekly "biggest mover" call-outs, so mid-pack reps stay in it.
- **Comeback wildcard:** one prize for the single biggest **% jump, any bucket** — lets a small territory steal the show (fits the "rally the dip" story).
- **Optional team layer:** RAD regions compete in parallel, so managers have skin in the game.
- ⚠️ More moving parts + more to track (leverages the live dashboard — see below).

**Recommendation:** run **Option A's engine with Option B's energy** — bucketed 50/50 scoring for fairness, points-with-quality-multiplier + a comeback wildcard for fun. Fair, engaging, protects the funnel, still easy to explain.
