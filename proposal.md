### Slide 4: Scoring Methodology

Copy and paste this into Copilot:

> Create a slide with a Two-Column layout.
> Set the slide title to: "How the scoring works"
> Set the subtitle to: "Here is how each territory is scored, and how the RAD group and the quality prize work"
> In the left column, add the bold heading "Scoring one territory" and format the following as bullet points:
> * The baseline is a territory's average enrollments over the last four quarters (Q3 2025 through Q2 2026). Take two thirds of that for the two month window. That is the number to beat.
> * Then look at what they actually enroll from August 1 to September 30.
> * We score two things: how many patients they added, and the percent they grew.
> * Each territory is ranked on both, inside its own size group. We average the two ranks into one score. If two tie, the higher percent growth wins.
> * The top two in each group get paid.
> * There is no ceiling. The more a territory grows past its baseline, the better it ranks.
> * We store the baseline by month, so we can switch the window later without rebuilding anything.
> In the right column, add the bold heading "The RAD group and the quality prize" and format the following as bullet points:
> * RADs are scored as one group, no size tiers. Same two measures, patients added and percent growth. Top two get paid. A RAD's baseline is just the sum of its territories.
> * The quality prize goes to the territory in each group whose enrollments most often reach TTP. You need at least 5 enrollments to qualify.
> * We settle this prize about 30 days after the contest ends, since it takes roughly a month for an enrollment to reach TTP. So this one is scored after October.
> * A territory can win the quality prize and still place first or second. It is a separate prize, not a trade off.
